# Study Guide: Electronic Configuration and Properties of Transition Elements

## 1. Core Lesson Summary

### Structure of Main Transition Elements (d-block)

#### **Electronic Configuration Pattern**
- In main transition elements, **electrons occupy the d-sublevel in sequence**
- Since the d-sublevel holds a maximum of **10 electrons**, main transition elements consist of **ten vertical columns**
- **First column**: Elements with $ns^2, (n-1)d^1$ configuration
- **Last column**: Elements with $ns^2, (n-1)d^{10}$ configuration
- The d-sublevel is filled sequentially from 1 to 10 electrons

#### **Group Classification**
The ten columns are organized into groups from left to right:
- **Group IIIB** (3), **Group IVB** (4), **Group VB** (5), **Group VIB** (6), **Group VIIB** (7)
- **Group VIII** (contains **three vertical columns**: 8, 9, 10)
- **Group IB** (1), **Group IIB** (2)

**Note**: Columns 8, 9, and 10 are collectively called **Group VIII** because these elements have **similar properties horizontally** rather than vertically.

### The Four Horizontal Series of Transition Elements

1. **First Transition Series** (4th period)
   - 3d sublevel filled successively
   - Starts with Scandium ($_{21}Sc$) and ends with Zinc ($_{30}Zn$)

2. **Second Transition Series** (5th period)
   - 4d sublevel filled successively
   - Starts with Yttrium ($_{39}Y$) and ends with Cadmium ($_{48}Cd$)

3. **Third Transition Series** (6th period)
   - 5d sublevel filled successively

4. **Fourth Transition Series** (7th period)
   - 6d sublevel filled successively

### Electronic Configuration Patterns and Stability

#### **Normal Filling Pattern (Hund's Rule)**
1. After $_{20}Ca$, there is **gradual filling** of the five 3d orbitals
2. **Single electrons** occupy each orbital first until $Mn$ ($3d^5$)
3. After manganese, **pairing of electrons** occurs in each orbital until $Zn$ ($3d^{10}$)

#### **Exceptional Configurations**
- **Chromium** ($_{24}Cr$): $[Ar] 4s^1 3d^5$ (not expected $4s^2 3d^4$)
- **Copper** ($_{29}Cu$): $[Ar] 4s^1 3d^{10}$ (not expected $4s^2 3d^9$)

**Reason for Exceptions**: Atoms become **more stable** when their outermost sublevels are:
1. **Completely filled** (like $3d^{10}$ in Cu)
2. **Half-filled** (like $3d^5$ in Cr)

### Oxidation States and Stability

#### **Examples of Stability Effects**

**Iron Oxidation:**
- $Fe$: $[Ar] 4s^2 3d^6$
- $Fe^{2+}$: $[Ar] 3d^6$ (less stable)
- $Fe^{3+}$: $[Ar] 3d^5$ (more stable - half-filled)
- **Result**: $Fe^{2+}$ is easily converted to $Fe^{3+}$

**Manganese Oxidation:**
- $Mn$: $[Ar] 4s^2 3d^5$
- $Mn^{2+}$: $[Ar] 3d^5$ (more stable - half-filled)
- $Mn^{3+}$: $[Ar] 3d^4$ (less stable)
- **Result**: $Mn^{2+}$ is NOT easily converted to $Mn^{3+}$

#### **General Oxidation State Patterns**

1. **All elements** of first transition series have **+2 oxidation state** (lose 4s electrons first), except scandium (+3 only)

2. **Oxidation states increase** from Sc to Mn (highest +7), then **decrease** to Zn (+2 only)

3. **Maximum oxidation state** equals the **group number**, except Group IB (coinage metals)

4. **Multiple oxidation states** exist because 4s and 3d sublevels are **close in energy**

### Definition of Transition Elements

**Transition Element**: An element in which the orbitals of **(d or f) sublevels** are occupied with electrons but **not completely filled** either in its **atomic state** or in **one of its oxidation states**.

#### **Classification Issues**

**Group IIB Elements** ($Zn$, $Cd$, $Hg$):
- **NOT considered transition elements**
- **Reason**: d-sublevel is completely filled ($d^{10}$) in both atomic state and +2 oxidation state

**Group IB Elements** ($Cu$, $Ag$, $Au$ - Coinage Metals):
- **ARE considered transition elements**
- **Reason**: d-sublevel is completely filled ($d^{10}$) in atomic state, but partially filled ($d^9$ or $d^8$) in oxidation states (+2 or +3)

## 2. Key Vocabulary and Definitions

| Term | Clear and Concise Definition |
|------|------------------------------|
| **d-block Elements** | Main transition elements where d-sublevel is being filled with electrons |
| **Hund's Rule** | Electrons occupy orbitals singly before pairing up in the same sublevel |
| **Half-filled Stability** | Extra stability when a sublevel contains exactly half its maximum electrons |
| **Coinage Metals** | Group IB elements (Cu, Ag, Au) historically used for making coins |
| **Oxidation State** | The apparent charge of an atom when electrons are assigned to more electronegative atoms |
| **Electronic Configuration** | The distribution of electrons among atomic orbitals in an atom |
| **Transition Element** | Element with partially filled d or f orbitals in atomic or ionic state |
